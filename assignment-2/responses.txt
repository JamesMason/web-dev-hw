Data can travel on the internet by being broken up into pieces of one size
(“packets”) and traveling that way, then being put back together at their
destination by instructions in the “wrapper” the “packets” came in.

The DNS is the service that changes web addresses from their human-friendly
versions (like google.com) into IP addresses (which are just numbers and dots)
for computers to use.

Cookies (on the internet) are pieces of information that get stored on your
browser keeping track of your activity on different websites. They can keep you
logged in or remembering where you left off or they can keep track of what you
look at to advertise to you. Cookies are delicious.

The three basic parts of a URL are the protocol, the domain name and the path.
