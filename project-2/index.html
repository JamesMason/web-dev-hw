A step-by-step guide to a robust art production cycle

This guide will walk you through, in broad strokes, some of the hard-won knowledge I've picked up on drawing techniques and materials over the last several decades.

Recommended materials

  1. Drawing paper of your choice for initial pencil drawing. 81/2 x 11 printer paper will suffice.
  2. A set of drawing pencils ranging from 9H to 8B to give a variety of hard/soft, light/dark shades.
  3. Kneaded and rubber erasers
  4. Blending stumps
  5. Pencil Sharpener
  6. Drawing board/clipboard
  7. Soft hair brush for removing eraser debris
  8. A scanner and printer.
  9. A computer with Photoshop
  10. Bienfang Graphics 360 translucent marker paper is 100% rag, and non-bleeding. It retains true color with permanent as well as watercolor markers and is also suitable for pencil, charcoal, pastels, and pen-and-ink. Cut into 81/2 x 11 sheets
  11. Sets of both Prismacolor markers and colored pencils, the more varied the colors the better, matching markers and pencils are helpful.

Before adding color


Step Materials needed from list
Always work from reference materials and try to draw what you see, not what you know. 1
Draw your subject with your lighter drawing pencils and begin to refine which lines work for you. 2
Do a tracing of the best lines that you want to keep instead of erasing the lines you don’t want.
Add shading and contrast and start picking out details. Use your erasers for drawing highlights more than for correction. Use the blending stumps to draw rounded surfaces without making hard lines, also great for shading. 3, 4
Once drawing is finished, scan your work and open up the image in photoshop on your computer. 8, 9
Adjust the size of the image to fit your 8 ½ x 11 area if needed and use the tools available to clean up your background of any smudges. Save as a high quality jpg. 9
Print your image on a sheet of marker paper, preferably on the non waxy side. This will give you a fully shaded and detailed drawing that you can render on. 10
Use the lightest prismacolor pencils to pick out your highlights first, such as the whites of eyes. This leaves a bright layer that you can get back if you accidently render over it. Use the markers to fill in larger areas, and use complimentary gradient colors that blend well together. Try this on a scratch piece of paper first. 11
Use the colored pencils to pick out fine details. You may have to do more shading and building of layers to get the look you want. Don’t be afraid of making mistakes, because you can always print out another drawing. 11
When finished, scan your work again and make any adjustments you need to in photoshop. The drawing can be digitally cut out and composited with other pieces of art at this point. 8, 9
